ASPIRING UI/UX DESIGNER & RESEARCHER

Project Overview

My Role:

UX designer designing an app for users to feed their sweet cravings with the proper search recommendations.

My Responsibilities:

Paper and digital wireframing, low and highfidelity prototyping, conducting interviews and usability studies, accessibility, iterating, and empathy for users.

The Product:

Sweet Tooth is an app where people can “feed their cravings”. It allows users to search their dessert needs without getting unwanted searches. It also allows private and small business owners to showcase their skills alongside bigger corporations.

The Problem:

When users were searching for desserts through google search/maps, they were getting recommendations like “McDonalds” because they sold sweets.

The Goal:

Create an app that allows users the satisfaction of finding their desired cravings, without the pain points of getting unwanted recommendations.

Project Duration

July 2023 – August 2023

User Research: Summer

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I’m designing for, as well as their needs. The primary user group was adults 18 and older with a big or small craving for something sweet.

This user group confirmed my initial assumptions about getting unwanted recommendations when searching for something sweet. During the research, it revealed that some users did not care for the other recommendations.

USER RESEARCH: PAIN POINTS

1

Unwanted Searches

User wanted searches that pertained to what it is they were searching for.

2

Only Big Companies

It only showed big companies. Multiple users wanted the option to see private and smaller businesses.

PERSONA: ANN JONES

PROBLEM STATEMENT:

Ann is a working adult who loves to eat sweets. She uses google maps to find places to fulfill her craving but gets frustated when getting unwanted searches.

USER JOURNEY MAP

Mapping Ann’s user journey map revealed how many people like her would benefit from having an app that only reveal searches related to what they want.

Usability Study: Findings

I conducted two rounds of usabilty studies. The findings in the first study helped me make necessary changes to help improve the the overall layout and user process. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed that users were more satisfied with design and functionality instead.

Round 1 Findings

1

Users want related searches

2

Users want the option to view private and smaller businesses

3

Users want to see the menu without having to click order

Round 2 Findings

1

Users found the app very easy to navigate

2

Users like the color and functionality of the app

2

Used Icons to help make navigation easier.

3

Use detailed imagery for the icons and descriptive text to help ensure the needs for all users.

Takeaways

IMPACT:

The app makes users feel as if they have a whole new variety of sweets to choose from. One quote from a real life peer: “I’ve never thought about how google search only shows you the bigger businesses. This app really allows me to explore more of what’s around me without the use of unwanted searches”.

WHAT I LEARNED:

While designing the Sweet Tooth app, I learned that your design can change base off of bias and usabilitty studies. The honest feedback from peer reviews and other professional UX designers influenced each iteration of the app’s design.

Next Steps

1

Conduct another round of usability studies to validate if the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed.

2

Continue making necessary changes to make the user flow and the design more comfortable.

3

Polish my skills with more practice to ensure I get better with every click I make, with each new design.
