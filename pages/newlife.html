PORTFOLIO

"New Life"

ASPIRING UI/UX DESIGNER & RESEARCHER

Project Overview

My Role:

UX designer designing a website for users to find a job opening and serves as a guide for fresh graduates and people who do not know.

My Responsibilities:

Paper and digital wireframing, low and high fidelity prototyping, conducting interiviews and usability studies, accessibility, iterating, and empathy for users.

The Product:

New Life is a website where people can find a job opening. This allows users to find a job opening, how to apply, what to wear and common questions companies will ask you

The Problem:

When the users are searching on how to find a job, they do not know what to do next after sending an application form or resume

The Goal:

Create a website that will help other people on getting a job. This is a guide on what to wear, job openings and most frequent questions asked by the interviewer.

Project Duration

July 2023 – August 2023

User Research: Summer

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I’m designing for, as well as their needs. The primary user group was adults 18 and older who’s looking for a job at a very young age.

This user group confirmed my initial assumptions about not knowing what to do before and after submitting the resume. During the research, it reveals that most users are having a hard time creating a resume, finding good clothes to wear and do not know how to answer the frequent questions asked by the interviewer.

USER RESEARCH: PAIN POINTS

1

Unwanted Searches

User wanted searches that pertained to what it is they were searching for.

2

Only Big Companies

It only showed big companies. Multiple users wanted the option to see private and smaller businesses.

Usability Study: Findings

I conducted two rounds of usabilty studies. The findings in the first study helped me make necessary changes to help improve the the overall layout and user process. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed that users were more satisfied with design and functionality instead.

Round 1 Findings

1

Users want to see the menu without having to click order

2

Users want the option to view private and smaller businesses

Round 2 Findings

1

Users found the app very easy to navigate

2

Users like the color and functionality of the app
